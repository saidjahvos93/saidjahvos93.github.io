The portrait in a contemporary context

In a world where everyone is taking selfies and putting themselves at the center of their universe, I started making selfportraits wherein I explore my own past and my effect on other people. My Identity to my past as a Belgian illustrator connects, for me to the image of Tintin, the young, adventurous reporter that sprang from the mind of Hergé. Placing the emotions of Tintin on top my own face puts them in stark contrast against each other. The dreamy clouds of charcoal fight with the highly stylized "ligne clair" and therefore, in my opinion, ridicule one another. The bluntness in wich the two esthetics talk to each other is quite comedic.

The culture of the face


The Japanese author Haruki Murakami once said "Your face is the only thing you'll never truly see". This realisation is something that in today's world of Instagram and Facebook feels lost on us. We are not aware how mysterious our own face is to us, and the fact that we spend so much time vainly constructing it. Reshaping it through cutting your hair, shaving or even plastic surgery while never truly seeing it via or own eyes feels quite funny to me.

Portrait of the absurd

I always strive to have an element of the absurd or the silly in my work. wich lies in opposition to what our usual understanding is of self representation. In the most common sense we trie to present ourselves als capabel,adult individuals. I prefer the idiotic. The quest to not be taken seriously seems contradictory to an artists career but to feels like a more natural state
